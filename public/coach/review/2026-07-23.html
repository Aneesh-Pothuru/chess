Chapter IV

The rules to play by

  • R1Any ...Nc6 → c3 now. Any ...Nb4 hitting Bd3 → Be2, then a3. This single rule refunds four losses. (London)
  • R2...e5 in Caro Exchange structures is banned until castled and developed. One-square pawn moves; breaks are a reward. (Caro-Kann)
  • R3Attacks need attackers ≥ defenders + 1. One piece near their king is a visit. Queen checks must do something concrete or they cost a tempo.
  • R4A capture ends after their recapture. Ask what the recapturing unit attacks next — pawns fork, files open, defenders vanish.
  • R5The two-question scan on every king move and every capture — can I be mated in one, can I mate in one. Five losses ended by skipping it, all with minutes on the clock.
  • R6Convert lines into rules. After every game: which drilled line almost appeared, and what rule covers the difference?

What is genuinely improving and worth protecting: the King's Indian (3W–0L), the Two Knights Caro when you remember it, fighting back from bad positions (Triss83 reached +4.6 from lost), and castle-by-8 in the wins. These rules are your existing drills applied at the board, on the six moves after contact.
