## Annotated game: Hamid137 (728) — pots1125 (705), Caro-Kann Exchange

**1.e4 c6 2.d4 d5 3.exd5 cxd5** — The Exchange Caro. Repertoire, and a structure you know:
symmetrical pawns, easy development, nothing to fear.

**4.Nc3 Nc6 5.Nge2 Nf6 6.Bg5 e6 7.Bxf6 Qxf6** — White hands over the bishop pair for no
reason. Recapturing with the queen is fine here; she's safe on f6 and eyes d4. Black is
already comfortable.

**8.Qd3 Bd6 9.a3 O-O** — Castled on move 9, one off the target but the right call over
9...anything-else. Note White's 9.a3 telegraphs long castling.

**10.O-O-O e5!** — Good chess. Kings on opposite wings means the center and the c-file are
Black's playground, and this break opens both. This is the best move of the day.

**11.Nxd5!? Qxf2** — White's trick: the knight grabs d5 and attacks the queen. Taking on
f2 keeps the material level and actually poses White problems. Brave and correct — but
notice the queen is now a long way from home, and Black's coordination hangs by threads.

**12.dxe5 Bxe5 13.h3 Rd8?!** — The move looks like textbook chess: rook to the open file,
piling on the pinned-looking knight. But it's the *f8*-rook, which does two quiet things:
it vacates f8 (the king's only flight square along the rank) and it puts a rook on d8
defended by exactly one piece — the knight on c6. The back rank is now held together by
one thread. 13...Nd4! instead (hitting the queen, blocking the file) or 13...Be6 keeps
everything glued.

**14.Ne7+!** — The thread gets cut. The knight gives check and simultaneously attacks the
c6 knight — the lone defender of d8. This is a *removal of the guard* dressed up as a
spite check.

**14...Nxe7??** — Played in about six seconds, and it's the losing move. The recapture
feels automatic: a free knight, +3 material, biggest advantage of the game. But ask the
question that was never asked: **what was the c6 knight doing?** It was the only piece
defending d8, where the rook sits attacked twice (queen on d3, rook behind on d1).
14...Kh8! was calm and fine: 15.Nxc6 bxc6 and material is level with a healthy game —
the queen on f2 is annoying, the c-file opens toward White's king. Black is not worse.

**15.Qxd8#** — Queen takes rook, backed by the d1 rook. With f8 vacated by move 13 and
the h7/g7/f7 pawns untouched, the king has no square. Back-rank mate — the pattern from
45 of the audit's 88 losses.

**The lesson to carry:** *a recapture is still a move.* It gets the same 5-second scan as
any other: what did my capturing piece stop defending, and can I be mated in one? Six
seconds of clock were spent on 14...Nxe7 with eight and a half minutes sitting unused.
